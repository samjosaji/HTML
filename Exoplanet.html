What are Exoplanets?

Exoplanets, or extrasolar planets, are planets that exist outside our solar system. They orbit stars other than our Sun and can vary widely in size, composition, and distance from their stars.

How are Exoplanets Discovered?

Scientists use various methods to detect exoplanets, including:

  • Transit Method: Observing the dimming of a star as a planet passes in front of it.
  • Radial Velocity: Measuring the star's wobble due to gravitational tugs from orbiting planets.
  • Direct Imaging: Taking pictures of planets directly by blocking the star's light.

Interesting Exoplanets

Here are some fascinating exoplanets that have been discovered:

  • Kepler-186f: An Earth-sized exoplanet in the habitable zone of its star.
  • HD 209458 b: The first exoplanet discovered to have an atmosphere.
  • Proxima Centauri b: The closest known exoplanet to Earth, potentially in the habitable zone.
